Students, alumni, parents and friends of UCLA certainly have a lot to be proud of.

Annual giving is the cornerstone of our institution. Each year, tens of thousands of Bruins join together to give back to UCLA. While UCLA Alumni contribute almost half of the total dollars to the UCLA Fund each year, Bruins of all affiliations have progressively rallied their support, empowering UCLA to make an indelible positive imprint on the world.

Over the past two years, our parent donors have increased by more than 10%, and friends of our university have increased by more than 30%.

Students and young alumni—those who have graduated within the last 10 years—have recognized the opportunities powered by Bruin generosity, and are proudly embracing their role as our next generation of philanthropic leaders. They have exhibited both increased volunteerism and donor participation, with young alumni donors increasing by more than 10% and student donors increasing by 20% since fiscal year 2013.

Read on to see how you and others empowered UCLA this fiscal year (July 1, 2014 – June 30, 2015).

Your Impact

Just a few examples of how your support is driving UCLA to make a difference.

0
Inventions

UCLA has a history of leading innovation in California that impacts the world. UCLA has an active portfolio of 2,950 inventions and at least 109 companies in California have licensed technologies developed at UCLA.

Top 10
Global University

UCLA is recognized around the globe as a world-class institution. U.S. News & World Report ranks UCLA 2nd among public universities. Its inaugural global rankings placed UCLA 8th in the world. UCLA also ranked 2nd among U.S. public universities in Shanghai Jiao Tong University's Academic Ranking of World Universities.

10,000+ Volunteers
Community Impact

UCLA is consistently ranked among the top service schools in the nation by Washington Monthly. The UCLA Volunteer Center works with over 10,000 volunteers each year at hundreds of community partner sites throughout the great Los Angeles area, with UCLA Volunteer Day serving as the nation's largest community participation event for new students.

Meet Navjot Sandhu

Navjot Sandhu '15 is a neuroscience major and a Chancellor's Student Circle donor. She gives because UCLA awakened her passion for medicine, and Bruin philanthropy supported the organizations that have then helped her. Navjot became a Medical Brigades volunteer in Honduras, bringing basic health care to people living in remote areas.

Meet Paul Chhean

Brought up with compassion as a core value, undergraduate Paul Chhean '15 is dedicated to helping others, especially children. He set his sights on a nursing career and transferred from a community college in Long Beach to UCLA School of Nursing, where he helps kids who are battling cancer. The avid Bruin fan gives because he's grateful every day for the opportunity the university has given him to make his dream a reality.

Meet Professor Pincetl

UCLA professor and environmental expert Stephanie Pincetl once faced down a bulldozer ripping out the chaparral near her childhood home in rural San Diego County. Today, she is a member of an all-star team of Bruin scientists working to make Los Angeles energy and water independent. She also is a loyal UCLA Fund donor. Why? Because Stephanie knows that many working as one can work wonders.

Meet Art and Lesilee Hylton

As the parents of two UCLA graduates, Art and Lesilee Hylton have a relationship with the university that stretches back to when their daughter became a student at the UCLA Lab School. The Hyltons engage with UCLA on many fronts and support areas across campus because the education in diversity and acceptance their child received at UCLA will stay with her for a lifetime.
