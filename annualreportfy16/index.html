Annual giving is the cornerstone of our institution. Each year, tens of thousands of Bruins join together to give back to UCLA. While UCLA Alumni contribute almost half of the total dollars raised from annual gifts each year, Bruins of all affiliations have progressively rallied their support, empowering UCLA to make an indelible positive imprint on the world.

Over the past two years, parent donors have been making more meaningful gifts, increasing their giving by more than 40% in comparison to last fiscal year. Friends of UCLA have also increased their philanthropic dollars by 70% from fiscal year 2015.

Students and young alumni—those who have graduated within the last 10 years—are also proudly embracing their role as our next generation of philanthropic leaders. They have exhibited both increased volunteerism and philanthropic giving, with young alumni increasing their total dollars raised by more than 35% and student donations increasing by more than 20% since fiscal year 2015.

Read on to see how you and others empowered UCLA this fiscal year (July 1, 2015 – June 30, 2016).

Community Impact

UCLA is consistently ranked among the top service schools in the nation by Washington Monthly. The UCLA Volunteer Center works with over 10,000 volunteers each year at hundreds of community partner sites throughout the great Los Angeles area, with UCLA Volunteer Day serving as the nation's largest community participation event for new students.

2015 Blue & Gold Challenge

A LOOK BACK AT OUR MOST SUCCESSFUL BLUE AND GOLD CHALLENGE EVER.

Bruins of all affiliations showed the strength of blue and gold pride during the Blue & Gold Challenge, which is our campus-wide, community-driven fundraising event to benefit all areas of UCLA’s campus.

Bruins well surpassed our 2,800 gift challenge and donated more than 5,000 gifts and pledges to UCLA. This robust participation led to record-breaking total dollars raised: more than $1.5 million over the campaign's 10 day run.
